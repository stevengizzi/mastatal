# Spec 02 — "From Root Tip to Wood Wide Web: Scales of Underground Intelligence"

**Priority:** Nice-to-have (do if time allows after Deliverable 1)
**Format:** Three connected panels on poster board, or three projected slides
**Estimated time:** 2-3 hours
**Audience:** PDC classmates

---

## Concept

A zoom-out diagram showing three nested scales of underground decision-making. Each scale has its own form of "intelligence," its own communication mechanism, and its own timescale. The visual tells a story of emergence: simple local rules → system-level coordination → community-level resource sharing.

This is the version that connects to Wohlleben / Simard / "Wood Wide Web" ideas and resonates with the PDC group's existing knowledge about guilds, companion planting, and food forest design.

---

## The Three Panels

### Panel 1: The Root Tip (millimeters, minutes)
**Title:** "The Local Agent"

A close-up of a single root tip showing:
- The four zones (cap, meristematic, transition, elongation)
- Tropism inputs as arrows coming in from the environment (gravity down, water to the left, obstacle ahead, etc.)
- Hormone gradients shown as color washes across the root cross-section (auxin concentration)
- Growth direction vector as the output arrow

**Key text:** "Each root tip makes its own local decision based on what it senses in its immediate environment. There is no central brain. The intelligence is distributed."

**Communication mechanism:** Hormone gradients within the root tip
**Timescale:** Minutes to hours
**Spatial scale:** Millimeters

---

### Panel 2: The Root System (centimeters to meters, days to weeks)
**Title:** "The Social Network"

A whole-plant root system view showing:
- Multiple root tips, each with their own growth directions
- Chemical signal clouds (exudates) emanating from root zones
- A neighboring plant's root system entering from the side
- Visual distinction between self-roots and non-self-roots (different colors)
- Zones of segregation (roots avoiding each other) and zones of intermingling (kin roots cooperating)

**Key text:** "Roots recognize self, kin, and stranger. They compete with strangers and cooperate with relatives. The language is chemical — over 100,000 different signal molecules."

**Communication mechanism:** Root exudates, volatile organic compounds
**Timescale:** Days to weeks
**Spatial scale:** Centimeters to meters

---

### Panel 3: The Mycorrhizal Network (meters to hundreds of meters, months to years)
**Title:** "The Wood Wide Web"

A landscape-scale view showing:
- Multiple trees of different sizes and species
- Underground fungal network (drawn as thin connecting lines between root systems)
- Arrows showing resource flow (carbon from nurse tree → seedling; phosphorus from fungus → tree)
- Defense signal propagation (one tree attacked → signal traveling through network → neighboring trees upregulating defenses)

**Key text:** "Fungal networks connect individual plants into a community. Resources and warning signals flow through the network. The oldest trees function as hubs."

**Communication mechanism:** Mycorrhizal hyphal networks, RNA exchange
**Timescale:** Months to years (network establishment); hours to days (signal propagation)
**Spatial scale:** Meters to hundreds of meters

---

## Connecting the Panels

Between each panel, draw a "zoom arrow" showing the scale transition. Include a note at the bottom that ties all three together:

**"Underground intelligence operates at three scales simultaneously. Each scale has its own sensors, its own communication system, and its own form of decision-making. The permaculture designer works at all three: choosing what to plant (Panel 3), where to plant it relative to neighbors (Panel 2), and shaping the soil conditions that individual root tips will navigate (Panel 1)."**

---

## Visual Style

- Keep illustrations schematic, not photorealistic
- Use consistent color coding across panels:
  - Green for living plant tissue
  - Brown for soil
  - Blue for water
  - Purple/pink for fungal networks
  - Yellow clouds for chemical signals
- Each panel should be readable independently but also work as a sequence

## Relationship to Deliverable 1

Deliverable 1 (the mixing console poster) is essentially a detailed version of Panel 1. If both deliverables are produced, they complement each other: Deliverable 1 is the deep dive into the root tip's decision architecture; Deliverable 2 is the zoom-out showing how that local decision-making fits into the larger picture.

For presentation: show Deliverable 2 first (big picture), then zoom into Deliverable 1 (how does a single root tip actually work?). This mirrors the permaculture design process: start with observation of the whole system, then drill into specifics.
