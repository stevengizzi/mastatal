The garden, the village & the household
Whole-systems permaculture design for the urban renter — Atlanta, GA · Steven & Jessie Gizzi · PDC 2026
Design for capacity, not infrastructure.
When your relationship to place is temporary, the most durable yields aren't plants or earthworks — they're skills, relationships, preserved food, and methodology. A permaculture design that ignores where your electricity comes from or where your trash goes isn't closing the loop. This design integrates five systems: food, water, energy, waste, and community.
Five household systems
Food
Vertical gardens, containers, fast perennials, community garden, foraging, preservation
Water
Rain barrels (legal in GA, tax credit up to $2,500), mulching, kitchen greywater reuse
Energy
Efficiency upgrades via GA Power (renters w/ landlord consent), portable solar, Simple Solar RECs
Waste
Worm bin, bokashi, CompostNow pickup ($20-35/mo), zero-waste kitchen, repair culture
Community
Shared meals, skill circles, childcare co-op, buying club, neighborhood web, movement orgs

Village blueprint: 5 layers
1
The kitchen table
2-4 families. Regular shared meals. Rotate hosting. Share surplus, swap ferments. The meal IS the infrastructure.
2
The skill share circle
6-10 people. Monthly: composting, fermentation, seed saving, propagation, medicine, NVC, repair, energy auditing.
3
The cooperative ring
8-15 families. Childcare co-op, buying club (via Fresh Harvest or farmers), tool share, e-cargo bike share. Consent-based governance.
4
The neighborhood web
Know neighbors. Share surplus. Front yard garden = conversation starter. Ambient community beyond your intentional circle.
5
The movement ecosystem
Food Well Alliance, Cooperative Atlanta, Concrete Jungle, Propel ATL, Drawdown Georgia. Give more than you take.
Governance
Consent-based decisions. Rounds for equal voice. Rotating roles. NVC for conflict. Regular retrospectives.

Phased timeline: all systems
Month 1
Fermentation + herbs + energy audit. Worm bin + CompostNow. Visit gardens, apply for plot. One neighbor meal. Map transport routes.
Month 3-6
Perennials + rain barrel + vertical expansion. Biweekly shared meals + first skill share. GA Power assessment. Thermal curtains + LEDs.
Year 1-2
First harvests + medicine cabinet. Childcare co-op forming. Seed saving. Energy baseline set. Compost loop closed. Recommit?
Year 2-3
Muscadine fruiting. Buying club + tool share operational. Seed library started. Repair cafe launched. Propagation practice.
Year 3-5
Full systems running. Deep network. Stay, buy, or move? If moving: take seeds, cultures, worm bin, verticals, solar, knowledge. Gift the rest.
