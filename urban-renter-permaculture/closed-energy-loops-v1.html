Closing the loops
Energy, water, waste and the dependency radius — a systems design for the urban renter
Steven Gizzi | PDC 2026 | Rancho Mastatal, Costa Rica
Transition is the fourth ethic.
Earth care. People care. Fair share. And: transition — the ethic that governs the journey between where you are and where you want to be. You don't need 200 acres to practice permaculture. You start where you are, with what you have, and design each move to bring you closer to a more integrated life. Every rain barrel installed, every loop closed, every dollar redirected locally is a meaningful act of transition.
Four closed loops for a renter household
Energy loop
reduce Thermal curtains, window film, draft stoppers, LEDs, smart strips, fans over AC, heated table (heat the person not the room), batch cooking
monitor Kill-A-Watt on every outlet. Track monthly kWh. Monitoring alone cuts use 5-15%
upgrade GA Power EASE: free insulation + duct sealing for renters (landlord consent). HEIP: 50% rebates. Biggest ROI = attic insulation + air sealing
generate 200-400W portable solar + LiFePO4 battery. Powers devices, lights, fans. Simple Solar RECs: match 100% usage ~$12.50/mo
HVAC = 40-50% of a Southern home's bill
Before fossil fuels, textile layers kept homes warm/cool. Thermal mass (water containers near windows) moderates swings with zero energy. Low-tech Magazine runs a website on 1-2.5W solar.
Water loop
harvest Rain barrel on existing downspout — legal in GA, no permit for outdoor use. Tax credit 25% up to $2,500 (HB 1069). Atlanta ~50 in/yr rainfall
conserve Mulch all soil (50-70% less evaporation). Drip irrigation via gravity from barrel. Drought-tolerant natives
reuse Manual greywater: pasta/rice/veggie water to plants. Shower bucket (2-5 gal/shower). Laundry to landscape if drain accessible + plant-safe soap
cycle Rain → barrel → garden → food → scraps → compost → soil → garden. The loop closes.
Zero plumbing modification needed
Every approach works without touching a pipe. Rain barrel on existing downspout. Greywater via manual bucket-carry. Zero permits.
Waste loop
divert Worm bin (scraps → castings → garden). Bokashi (handles meat/dairy). CompostNow pickup $20-35/mo, compost returned after 4 months
reduce Buying club via Fresh Harvest (reusable bins, 7+ years in circulation). Bulk purchasing. Zero-waste kitchen: preservation, fermentation, nose-to-tail
repair Monthly repair cafe in skill share circle. Mending. Extending product life. Currently a gap in Atlanta — opportunity to build
cycle Scraps → compost → garden → food → kitchen. Packaging: reuse or refuse. Broken: repair or recover
Composting exempt from GA regulation
Food Well Alliance + Turner Environmental Law Clinic provide GA-specific guidelines. Drop-off at Browns Mill + Wylde Center.
Transport loop
walk Choose housing by proximity. The most sustainable trip is the one you don't take
bike Protected lanes arriving 2026 (Midtown, Decatur, Grant Park). Beltline Southside Trail 2026. E-cargo bike replaces second car
transit MARTA rail + bus. New BRT A-Line 2026: 14 stops, electric buses, dedicated lanes. Bikes welcome all trains, all times
share E-cargo bike shared in cooperative ring. Car-share when needed. Buying club consolidates trips
Bike + MARTA = full ITP coverage
Every bus has front bike rack (2 bikes). Combine cycling + transit for car-free living across the metro.
Shrinking the dependency radius
Circle 1 — The household
Produce, preserve, repair, manage yourself. Fermentation, medicine, composting, energy monitoring, mending. Smallest circle, highest sovereignty.
Circle 2 — The cooperative ring (8-15 families)
Shared tools, bulk food, cooperative childcare, skill exchange, shared transport. Collective spending = economic gravity. The flywheel turns.
Circle 3 — The city
Fresh Harvest, CompostNow, community gardens, MARTA, local credit union, Cooperative Atlanta. Each local dollar strengthens the network.
Circle 4 — The bioregion
Georgia-grown food. Regional materials (Southern pine, red clay). State networks (Georgia Organics, Food Well Alliance).
Circle 5 — Global (minimize)
Some electronics, some medicines. Far less than you think. Track quarterly. Goal: steady, measurable shift inward.
Creative engineering: think big, source local
DIY solar air heater
Salvaged glass + aluminum cans + plywood + foam. PV-powered fan. $50-200. Offsets 30-70% daytime heating in Atlanta winters. Fully removable.
Thermal mass + phase change
Freeze water containers overnight, fan-cool by day. Or: water jugs near sunny windows as passive heat batteries. Zero moving parts, zero energy.
Stand-alone solar station
200-400W panel + LiFePO4 battery + inverter. Powers devices, lights, fans. Energy literacy through visibility: see the sun-to-use pipeline.
Kotatsu / heated table
Heat the person, not the room. Insulated table + small element + blanket. Fraction of whole-room energy. Ancient Japanese design, modern efficiency.
AI as sovereignty tool
The Agricultural Revolution traded personal sovereignty for specialization. Ten thousand years later, the knowledge for self-sufficiency is scattered across dozens of professional disciplines. This is the barrier to permaculture adoption: not land, not money — accessible, integrated knowledge.

AI collapses this barrier. This project is proof: a composer at a two-week PDC producing a whole-systems design — with cited regulations, yield timelines, engineering solutions — for a city he hasn't moved to yet. The output compounds: teach others without the AI. The first household shares with the next ten.
Systems timeline
Month 1
Energy audit. Worm bin + CompostNow. Rain barrel research. Map bike/MARTA routes. First local purchases.
Month 3-6
Rain barrel. Curtains + LEDs. GA Power assessment. Buying club. E-cargo bike. Manual greywater.
Year 1
15-25% energy cut. Compost loop closed. First solar. Repair cafe. Track dependency radius.
Year 2-3
DIY solar heater. Thermal mass. 50%+ food from GA. Car trips -60%. Share model.
Year 3-5
All loops running. Deep network. Take knowledge, methodology, skills. Build the next one faster.
